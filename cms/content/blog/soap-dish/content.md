I realize this is not the most exciting project, but it really has made an improvement in my daily life. 

A few months ago, I switched to using bars for all of my soap/shampoo/conditioner needs. Honestly this change has been great for me, my skin and hair feel much better than the bottled soaps I was using before, but the problem with bar soap is there's not a great place to put it in the shower.

# My soap dish journey
At the time of switching to bar soap, I had quickly designed a stand for linoleum carving a few weeks prior that wasn't getting much use. I figured I'd use that at my soap holder, to prevent the gummy deterioration of the soap bars left on the side of the tub.

![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-1.jpg)

The soap would still get stuck to the printed part instead of the tub so it had all of the same issues, but maybe just a little easier to remove.

I finally decided enough was enough and I would make a real soap dish.

In my first attempt, I wanted to be cheeky and make it infinitely expandable in the impossible case I would end up needing to shelter more than 3 bars of soap. (or rather to just allow other people to download as many as they needed).

![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-2.jpg)
![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-3.jpg)

That night I tested this design (i.e. I took a shower) and learned that the soap bars were far too slippery to grab out of the slots when they were wet.

I did a quick redesign that still allowed for drainage, while keeping the bars easily accessible. I also ensured the design would still work when the soap bars become smaller near the end of their life.

![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-4.jpg)
![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-5.jpg)
![](/cms-assets/blog/soap-dish/image-6.jpg)

The design seems to work well. You can toss the soap into the slot in many different orientations, which makes it feel usable enough to forget it's even there.


# The end of scrappy summer

This is the seventh summer project which, since the goal was a project every 7 days that took no more than 7 hours, feels like a fitting end to this experiment. 

I learned a lot by doing this weekly project challenge but it does feel like I am unable to take on some of the more ambitious projects that I am interested in at the moment.

There is certainly something to "shipping" every week, but I will try to bring that mindset to shipping pieces of a larger cohesive goal.
